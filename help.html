The Triplesec Diary

What is "The Triplesec Diary?"

It is an encrypted diary/journal that runs in your web browser

How Does It Work?

The Triplesec Diary uses a javascript library called triplesec.js to encrypt your diary before writing it out on your computer. The app stores your encrypted diary in your browser's local storage.

How Do I Know It is Secure?

At some point we have to trust the encryption software writers.

For this apps part, it never sends your diary across the internet, encrypted or otherwise. It never writes the unecrypted text of your diary to localstorage, it only keeps it in memory. Whenever you save, the content of your diary is encrypted, then written to your browser's local storage.

Provided you aren't actively using your diary and your computer hasn't been compromized in some manner, you should be safe.

Can the NSA Listen In?

Your actual diary is never sent across the internet, so there is nothing to eavesdrop on. Of course, since the diary must be decrypted to show it to you on your screen, nothing prevents a trojan from scraping your screen to read your diary. Also, if you have a trojan, nothing prevents it from simply listening to your keystrokes and recording your password.

Practice good sanitation

What are the Passphrase Requirements?

The only requirement is that the passphrase is at least one letter, number or symbol long. In general, the longer, the better. A good technique is to use four or more random dictionary words to create a long, easy to remember password. Another good technique that can be used on its own, or in combination with the prior suggestion, is to use a password manager.
